IRRAS Data & Maps
Funded by the Gates Foundation, IRRAS is a 4-year project (2011 - 2015) that investigates promising agricultural technologies for stress-prone rainfed rice farmers in Bihar. To do this, IRRAS uses a research pipeline of on-station, on-farm, and village trials. This site offers real-time project monitoring data to facilitate comparisons between trial technologies and traditional farmer technologies related to rice, wheat, lentil and chickpea. Visit the IRRAS Homepage and IRRAS Facebook page for more information about project objectives and activities.

IRRAS Mapping Tool The IRRAS project area includes more than 65 villages in several flood- and drought-prone districts of Bihar, one of India’s poorest states. Learn more about the IRRAS project objectives and download related resources at: http://irras.in

The data on this site is collected on a weekly basis from farmers participating in the IRRAS project. These farmers include: 1) progressive farmers, who are tending a project-sponsored demonstration plot and adjacent control plot that compare improved crop varieties and cultivation practices with traditional farmer varieties and practices; and 2) random farmers, who are more representative of the typical stress-prone rainfed rice farmers of Bihar.

With this data, we hope to provide an accurate idea about the local costs of production of our recommended crop varieties and related cultivation practices as compared to the traditional varieties and practices. All costs are reported in Indian Rupees.
